About

I am a student developer with a strong interest in building intelligent systems that are both efficient and privacy-aware. My work primarily focuses on federated learning, where I explore how decentralized models can collaborate without sharing raw data.

Recently, I have been working on designing a trust-based adaptive scoring mechanism in federated learning environments. This approach aims to improve model reliability by dynamically evaluating client contributions and reducing the impact of unreliable or malicious participants.

I enjoy breaking down complex systems into clear, functional mechanisms and translating them into practical implementations. Alongside this, I continue to strengthen my foundation in software engineering, machine learning, and system design.
